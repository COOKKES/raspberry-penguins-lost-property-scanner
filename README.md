# Raspberry Penguins 2026

## Project Brief

Our idea is to decrease the amount of lost property in schools, colleges, universities and nurseries!

## Research

We decided to explore the sustainability category. We investigated how many items were lost each year in schools, on average children lose an estimated 9 items. With 10 million school children in the UK, this adds up to 90 million lost items every single year, just in schools alone.

Parents spend significant amounts replacing lost items, with our research surveys indicating many spend £20- £100, with jumpers, cardigans, lunch boxes and water bottles being the most lost, totalling millions of pounds nationwide each year. Last year alone our school threw out 36 odd shoes!

We decided to make a difference!

## Our Product- Lost Property Scanner

Our program creates a unique QR Code for each student, which is printed on a label they can stick onto items they take to school. We have researched polyester waterproof labels that will stick onto all items and go through the washing machine.

If the student loses an object, it will be scanned and will show who the item belongs to, their form tutor and parents email address. The lost property will then be returned to the child. This will save time for teachers and help the environment by reducing waste.

Our main target audience is school children; however, this will help parent's keep track of their child's belongings.

## How Does This Help Our Planet?

1. Sustainable world: Factory's won't need to make as many replacements and there will be less pollution. There will be less plastic in the ocean each year and landfill from unwanted items.
2. Future organisations: We will change the way that schools, colleges, universities and nursery's deal with lost property waste!
3. Empowered consumers: This will have a positive impact on children taking care of their belongings and becoming more responsible.
4. Healthier humans: This will help peoples mental health. There will be less stress and money worries for families that keep having to buy replacement items.

## Sustainability

All our labels for the QR code are recyclable, waterproof and cheap to print. Our E-ink display screen only uses power when the image changes. This results in up to 99% less energy usage compared to standard screens, offering a long battery life and lower carbon footprints.

## Inclusivity

The fonts on the screen are dyslexic friendly. We have made the screen easy to understand with a clear layout and big writing to help people read the results. We have also used colours that are appropriate for people that are colour blind.

To make the project more inclusive in the future we would like to add in text to speech for those who are blind. We would also like a bigger button on the scanner.

## Scalability

We used a portable charger and small screen so the lost property scanner can be used anywhere. The student database is on the Raspberry Pi, an improvement would be to have a real time database in the cloud so you could have multiple scanners around different areas of the schools or other educational settings. It could also be used around the world.

## Using Our Project

We have started to use our project at our school and have had excellent feedback. We really do believe it will make a big difference to the amount of lost property we have.

We hope you like our project as much as we do!
